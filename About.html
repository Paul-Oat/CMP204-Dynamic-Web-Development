About


Overview

The White Stripes are an American Rock Duo of Jack and Meg White. Formed in 1997 they rose to popularity in 2002 with "Elephant" and "White Blood Cells" proving poular with Music Critics and Fans alike. The song Seven Nation Army with its easily identifiable guitar Rift proved popular with the Italian Football Fans at The 2006 World Cup.

Jack White

Jack Anthony White born July 9th 1975 is best known for bands such as The Racontoures and The White Stripes. Born in Detroit Michigan to a Polish mother and Scottish-Canidian father. In 1997 he formed the band The White Stripes and two months later performed their first show at the Gold Dollar in Detroit

Meg White

Megan Martha White is the main drummer. Born on December 10, 1974, the daughter of Catherine and Walter Hackett White Jr in Detroit. She trained in being a Chef and met Jack whist working behind a Bar in Michigan. Jack refers to Megs Druming as "a breath of fresh air"
